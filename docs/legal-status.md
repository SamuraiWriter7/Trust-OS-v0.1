# Legal Status

## 1. Overview

Trust OS v0.1 is an experimental, non-monetary trust layer.

It is designed to record and update contributor-level trust states based on gratitude records, trust events, and policy-defined signals.

Trust OS does not define legal rights, financial claims, payment obligations, ownership rights, securities, or transferable assets.

In short:

```text
Trust OS records trust signals.
Trust OS does not create legal entitlement.
```

---

## 2. Non-Legal Status

Trust OS v0.1 is not a legal system.

It does not provide:

- legal identity verification
- legal credit scoring
- legal reputation certification
- contractual enforcement
- statutory rights
- regulatory compliance guarantees
- court-recognized proof by itself

Trust OS may be used as a technical or conceptual record layer, but any legal effect depends on external laws, contracts, institutions, or agreements.

---

## 3. Non-Monetary Status

Trust OS v0.1 is non-monetary.

It does not define:

- payments
- royalties
- revenue sharing
- token rewards
- transferable points
- securities
- financial instruments
- tax treatment
- debt obligations
- dividend rights

A trust score is not money.  
A trust event is not a payment.  
A trust profile is not a financial account.

Trust OS only records structured trust information.

---

## 4. No Automatic Royalty Entitlement

Trust OS may be referenced by Royalty OS in the future.

However, a Trust OS record does not automatically create a royalty claim.

For example:

```text
High trust score ≠ automatic royalty entitlement
```

Royalty OS may choose to use Trust OS data as one input for value allocation, but that decision must be defined by a separate Royalty OS policy.

Trust OS provides trust data.  
Royalty OS defines allocation logic.

---

## 5. No Ownership Rights

Trust OS does not create ownership rights.

A contributor may receive trust signals for a contribution, but this does not automatically mean the contributor owns:

- the project
- the protocol
- the output
- the derivative work
- the implementation
- the repository
- the surrounding ecosystem

Ownership, licensing, copyright, and usage rights must be handled separately through applicable licenses, contracts, or legal frameworks.

---

## 6. No Transferable Token

Trust OS v0.1 does not define a transferable token.

A trust score should not be treated as:

- a cryptocurrency
- a security token
- a utility token
- a tradable asset
- a redeemable voucher
- a financial product

Trust OS records are intended to be informational and non-transferable unless a future specification explicitly defines otherwise.

---

## 7. No Credit Bureau Function

Trust OS is not a financial credit bureau.

It does not evaluate a person’s ability to repay debt.  
It does not determine loan eligibility.  
It does not provide consumer credit reports.  
It does not define financial creditworthiness.

The word “trust” in Trust OS refers to contribution-based trust within a project, community, protocol, or value stack.

It should not be confused with financial credit scoring.

---

## 8. Experimental Status

Trust OS v0.1 is experimental.

It is intended for:

- conceptual design
- technical prototyping
- open discussion
- non-monetary reputation modeling
- contributor trust tracking
- future integration with Gratitude OS and Royalty OS

It should not be treated as a production-ready legal, financial, or compliance system.

---

## 9. Advisory Nature of Trust Scores

Trust scores are advisory signals.

They may help a community, project, or protocol understand accumulated contribution patterns.

However, trust scores should not be treated as absolute truth.

Possible limitations include:

- incomplete data
- biased gratitude records
- inflated trust events
- missing contributions
- subjective policy choices
- community-specific interpretation
- outdated trust history
- accidental or malicious manipulation

Trust OS should be used with human judgment, governance review, and transparent policy design.

---

## 10. Policy-Defined Interpretation

Trust OS does not define one universal meaning of trust.

Each implementation may define its own trust policy.

For example, a project may define:

```yaml
trust_policy:
  version: "0.1"
  interpretation:
    overall_score:
      meaning: "General contribution-based trust within this project."
    protocol_design:
      meaning: "Trust related to protocol design contributions."
    feedback_quality:
      meaning: "Trust related to useful feedback and correction."
```

The meaning of a trust score depends on the policy that produced it.

Trust OS provides structure.  
Policy provides interpretation.

---

## 11. Need for Context

Trust profiles should always be interpreted in context.

A trust score may be meaningful only within:

- a specific project
- a specific repository
- a specific protocol
- a specific community
- a specific governance framework
- a specific time period

A score generated in one context should not automatically be reused in another context without review.

---

## 12. Privacy and Identity Considerations

Trust OS may support pseudonymous or privacy-aware contributor identities.

A contributor reference may represent:

- a public identity
- a pseudonymous identity
- an organization
- an AI agent
- a collective
- a project-level contributor

Implementations should avoid exposing unnecessary personal information.

Recommended principle:

```text
Record enough to preserve traceability.
Do not expose more identity data than necessary.
```

---

## 13. Auditability Requirement

If Trust OS records are used for important decisions, they should be auditable.

A trust event should ideally preserve:

- source reference
- contributor reference
- trust delta
- affected dimensions
- timestamp
- reason
- policy version
- update history

This helps prevent arbitrary or unexplained trust changes.

---

## 14. Dispute and Correction

Trust OS v0.1 does not define a full dispute resolution system.

However, implementations should consider future support for:

- correction requests
- trust event reversal
- disputed source records
- governance review
- audit comments
- appeal processes
- versioned updates

Trust records should not become irreversible judgments.

Trust must remain correctable.

---

## 15. Avoiding Harmful Use

Trust OS should not be used to create rigid social ranking systems.

Possible harmful uses include:

- excluding new contributors
- freezing early reputation advantages
- punishing dissent
- automating social judgment
- creating opaque reputation hierarchies
- using trust scores outside their intended context

Trust OS should support circulation, recognition, and accountability.

It should not become a tool of control.

---

## 16. Relationship to Gratitude OS

Gratitude OS records appreciation for specific contributions.

Trust OS aggregates those gratitude records into trust profiles.

However:

```text
gratitude mark ≠ legal claim
trust event ≠ payment right
trust profile ≠ ownership record
```

Gratitude OS and Trust OS are both non-monetary layers in v0.1.

---

## 17. Relationship to Royalty OS

Royalty OS may later reference Trust OS data.

However, Trust OS itself does not define royalty allocation.

Recommended boundary:

```text
Trust OS = trust data
Royalty OS = allocation policy
```

Any monetary, quasi-monetary, or contractual effect must be defined outside Trust OS.

---

## 18. No Legal Advice

This specification is not legal advice.

It is a technical and conceptual document for an experimental trust-layer protocol.

Anyone implementing Trust OS in a legal, financial, employment, governance, or commercial context should consult qualified legal, compliance, and domain experts.

---

## 19. Summary

Trust OS v0.1 is:

```text
experimental
non-monetary
non-transferable
policy-driven
context-dependent
advisory
trust-oriented
```

Trust OS v0.1 is not:

```text
a legal identity system
a credit bureau
a financial product
a payment system
a royalty entitlement system
a securities mechanism
a legal ownership registry
```

The core boundary is simple:

```text
Trust OS records accumulated trust.
It does not create legal or financial rights by itself.
```

Trust begins as recognition.  
Recognition may become credibility.  
Credibility may later inform value circulation.

But Trust OS itself remains a non-monetary trust layer.
